Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about conveyancing fees, disbursements, and instructions.

What is conveyancing?

Conveyancing is the legal process of transferring the ownership of real estate property from one person or entity to another. It involves drafting contracts, conducting local searches, verifying titles, and handling the mortgage funds transfer.

What are disbursements?

Disbursements are third-party fees that your solicitor must pay to other organizations on your behalf (such as local search authorities or HM Land Registry). These are standard government or search agency costs and are separate from the solicitor's legal service fees.

Why is leasehold conveyancing more expensive?

Leasehold properties require significantly more work from the solicitor. They must inspect the lease agreement, correspond with the landlord/freeholder, verify service charges, and arrange ground rent notices, which incurs a standard leasehold supplement fee.

Are these actual legal quotes?

These generated figures are demo estimates for testing. Once real solicitors confirm details of your transaction, they will issue a formal, binding fixed legal fee quote, subject to survey and contract instructions.
